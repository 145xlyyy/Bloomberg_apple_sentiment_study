2×2 Contingency Table

Directional Synchronization

Because sentiment intensity shows little explanatory power, the analysis further examines whether the direction of sentiment aligns with the direction of stock price movements. Sentiment is classified into positive or negative categories based on the sentiment score, while stock performance is categorized as price increase or price decrease. These two binary variables are organized into a 2×2 contingency table to compare how often positive sentiment coincides with rising prices and negative sentiment coincides with falling prices. The results show that positive sentiment is more frequently associated with stock price increases, while negative sentiment tends to coincide with price decreases. This pattern indicates that social media sentiment may reflect the general market mood during iPhone launch weeks, even if it cannot explain the magnitude of returns.
