Chi-Square Test

Statistical Significance

To statistically test the relationship identified in the contingency table, a Chi-square test of independence is conducted. The test evaluates whether sentiment direction and stock movement direction are independent of each other. The results produce a Chi-square statistic of approximately 5.93 with a p-value of 0.0149, which is below the conventional 0.05 significance level. This leads to the rejection of the null hypothesis of independence, indicating that the direction of social media sentiment and the direction of AAPL’s stock movement are statistically associated. The Phi coefficient (φ ≈ 0.35) further suggests a moderate relationship between the two variables. In other words, while sentiment intensity may not strongly influence returns, the tone of market discussions tends to move in the same direction as the stock price during major product launch periods.
