Pearson Correlation Analysis

Quantifying Linear Strength

The Pearson correlation test is used to examine whether there is a linear relationship between social media sentiment intensity and AAPL’s weekly stock returns. In this study, the sentiment score constructed from Twitter data is compared with AAPL’s weekly return and abnormal return during the event windows. The Pearson coefficient (r) measures the strength and direction of a linear association between the two variables. Results based on the 50 event-week observations show a weak positive correlation (r ≈ 0.16–0.19) with p-values above 0.18, indicating that sentiment intensity does not significantly explain variations in weekly returns. This suggests that although social media discussions increase around product launches, the magnitude of price changes for a mega-cap company like Apple is influenced more by broader market conditions and investor expectations than by the intensity of online sentiment alone.
